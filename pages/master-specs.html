What actually matters

How to Use This Data

Not all specs are created equal. Here's what to prioritize when making a buying or repair decision.

Cycle Life > Headline Capacity

A device rated for 4,000+ cycles (LFP) at 80% state of health will deliver far more usable energy over its lifetime than a device with 500 cycles (NMC) even if the headline watt-hour number is similar. LFP chemistry (LiFePO4) typically delivers 6-10x the cycle life of NMC. Compare cycle life × capacity, not just capacity alone.

Surge Output > Continuous Output

Motor-driven devices (well pumps, refrigerators, window AC, power tools) briefly draw 2-5x their rated wattage at startup. A 4,000W continuous / 8,000W surge rating handles these real-world loads where a 3,000W continuous / 3,000W surge unit will trip. Match the surge rating to the largest motor-driven load you intend to run.

Chemistry Temperature Sensitivity

Different chemistries respond very differently to cold and heat: LFP is rated to -20°C but pre-warming is recommended; NMC degrades faster above 45°C; Ni-MH has the narrowest sweet-spot and drops significant capacity below freezing. Always check the operating temperature range for your climate.

Solar Input & MPPT Range

The solar wattage spec tells you how fast the unit can recharge off-grid, but the MPPT voltage window determines what panels it can actually use. A 12-120V dual MPPT accepts nearly any panel configuration; a narrower 12-60V unit won't pair well with high-voltage commercial panels. Match panel VOC (open-circuit voltage) to the MPPT window.

Warranty & Repair Availability

Nearly every power station brand offers a 5-year warranty, but real-world support quality varies dramatically. Brands with documented error codes, published disassembly procedures, and replacement-part ecosystems give you real options beyond warranty expiration. A device that bricks at year 6 with no repair path is effectively a 5-year device regardless of original cycle-life promise.

Weight & Portability Tradeoffs

Higher-capacity units are heavy — 70 lbs for the top-tier power stations is not unusual. Plan your transport: wheeled cases exist for some models; other units are designed primarily for fixed installation. The price per watt-hour metric ($/Wh) normalizes comparisons: EcoFlow Delta Pro 3 sits at ~$0.98/Wh, while smaller budget units range from $0.70-1.20/Wh depending on the generation.
