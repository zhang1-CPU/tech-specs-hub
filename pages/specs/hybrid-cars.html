High-Voltage Safety — Read Before Proceeding: Toyota HV battery packs operate at 200–360 VDC. Honda IMA at ~160 VDC. Ford PHEV at 150–360 VDC. Lethal voltages remain present for up to 10 minutes after power-off. Always wait ≥ 10 minutes, disconnect the 12V battery negative terminal first, and verify pack voltage is below 50 VDC with a CAT III-rated multimeter before any physical contact with HV components.

Honda's 2-Motor i-MMD system uses the ICE primarily as a generator, with the traction motor driving the wheels. This differs from Toyota's planetary gearset (THS-II) architecture. When diagnosing Honda HEV faults, look for IMA/IPU codes rather than P0A80.

Why Charge to 100%? (LFP Voltage Curve)

LFP has a "plateau-style" voltage curve:

  • 10% to 90%: voltage almost flat (3.2V-3.3V)
  • BMS cannot estimate by voltage alone
  • Must reach 100% voltage spike to reset calibration
