EcoFlow Error Code Reference (E0–E7)

Cross-referenced from EcoFlow BMS diagnostics, engineering documents, and verified user field reports.

Code Severity Description / Trigger Action
E0 Critical AC Over-temperature Inverter thermal protection triggered. Reduce load immediately. Improve ventilation — ensure at least 12 inches of clearance on all sides. Let unit cool for 10–15 minutes before restarting. Do not stack or enclose in confined spaces.
E1 Warning Battery Over-temperature Move unit to shade. Stop charging. If in direct sun, relocate indoors. Wait for battery temperature to drop below 45°C (113°F). For cold-weather operation below 0°C, pre-warm battery with a short AC charge cycle first.
E2 Warning DC Over-temperature DC port or USB-C PD rail overheating. Reduce USB/DC load. Disconnect high-draw DC devices (Anderson, RV port). Inspect for faulty cables or short circuits on the DC side.
E3 Warning AC Overload (X-Boost active) Disconnect loads exceeding the 4,000W continuous rating. X-Boost handles short spikes up to 8,000W, but sustained overload triggers this code. Verify total connected wattage before retrying. Inductive motor-driven loads (well pumps, AC compressors) may need a soft-start kit.
E4 Warning Input Over-current Charging source wattage exceeds rated input. Reduce charging current — if using the EV X-Stream adapter (3,000W), switch to the standard 1,800W AC cable. Test with a known-good, dedicated 15A wall circuit.
E5 Info Communication Error Internal BMS-to-inverter handshake fault. Perform a full reboot: power off, disconnect all loads, hold power button for 10 seconds. If persistent, update firmware via the EcoFlow mobile app. Expansion-pack connector reseat may also resolve.
E6 Critical Battery Cell Failure One or more LFP cells has failed or drifted out of voltage-tolerance window (±10mV). Contact EcoFlow support immediately. Do not continue to charge or discharge. Likely requires RMA — covered under the 5-year warranty.
E7 Warning Fan Blocked / Fan Error Cooling fan stalled or intake/exhaust obstructed. Check and clear fan vents. Inspect fan operation visually. Do not operate without cooling — thermal runaway risk with LFP is low, but sustained overheating damages cells. Warranty service may be needed if fan has mechanically failed.

Troubleshooting Guide

Step-by-step diagnostic procedures for the most common user-reported issues.

Unit Won't Turn On

  1. Verify battery state of charge — if below 5%, connect to AC first.
  2. Hard reset: Disconnect all loads, then hold the main power button for 10 continuous seconds.
  3. After the reset, reconnect AC power and observe the LCD wake-up sequence.
  4. Check that expansion-pack connectors are fully seated (if used).
  5. If still unresponsive and E6 appears, contact EcoFlow support for RMA.

Slow Charging from AC

  1. Verify a dedicated 15A wall circuit is being used — shared circuits with other high-draw devices throttle charging.
  2. Confirm the EV X-Stream adapter (3,000W) is correctly mated if using the faster mode.
  3. Check for E4 (input over-current) — the unit self-limits to protect the inverter.
  4. Ambient temperature below 0°C or above 45°C reduces charge rate — this is normal LFP thermal behavior.
  5. If charge rate stays below 1,000W on a known-good circuit, test with a replacement AC cable.

AC Output Shuts Off Unexpectedly

  1. Check if E3 (overload) was displayed — calculate total connected wattage.
  2. Inductive motor-driven loads (well pumps, refrigerators, window AC) draw 3-5x rated wattage at startup. Use a soft-start kit for large compressors.
  3. X-Boost (8,000W) handles short spikes — but sustained draw above 4,000W triggers shutdown.
  4. Verify ventilation — E0 thermal shutdown is the #2 cause of unexpected cutouts.

Fan Always On at Low Speed

  1. The Delta Pro 3 uses variable-speed thermal-controlled fans. Continuous low-speed operation often indicates a thermal sensor calibration drift.
  2. Reboot: Power off, disconnect all loads, hold power button 10 seconds, reapply AC.
  3. Check firmware version — v2.2.0 addressed several fan-curve calibration issues.
  4. Fan noise in cold temperatures below 32°F (0°C) is normal — bearings take 5–10 minutes to warm up.

App Not Connecting

  1. Put the unit into pairing mode by pressing and holding the Wi-Fi / Bluetooth button for 3 seconds.
  2. Reset Wi-Fi via the EcoFlow app: Settings → Network → Reset Wi-Fi.
  3. Ensure phone is on the same 2.4GHz network (5GHz-only SSIDs are not supported for pairing).
  4. Disable VPN, private-DNS, and any ad-blockers during pairing.
  5. Update the EcoFlow app and the unit firmware to the latest versions.

LCD Backlight Won't Wake

  1. Press any button twice — first press wakes the system, second press activates the backlight.
  2. If unresponsive, perform a 10-second power-button reset.
  3. This is a documented firmware quirk in early production units — resolved by updating to v2.2.0 or newer.

Known Issues & Firmware Notes

Documented hardware and software issues. Most resolved by firmware update to v2.2.0 or newer.

X-Boost Overshoot Bug (v2.1.3)

Firmware v2.1.3 had a documented X-Boost overshoot bug where surge-current detection was too aggressive, occasionally triggering E3 (overload) on devices that were well within rating. This was particularly common with well pumps and older compressor-driven refrigerators.

Fixed in v2.2.0. Update via the EcoFlow mobile app.

Fan Noise in Cold Temperatures

Below 32°F (0°C), the cooling fan bearings take 5–10 minutes to warm up and may produce audible bearing noise during initial spin-up. This is a normal thermal characteristic of the bearing grease and is not a hardware failure.

The noise diminishes as the unit warms to operating temperature and is expected behavior per EcoFlow engineering documentation.

Battery Gauge Drops from 100% to 95% Quickly

Some early production units exhibit a calibration drift where the state-of-charge gauge drops rapidly from 100% to 95%, then stabilizes. This is a cosmetic BMS-calibration offset, not actual cell degradation.

Resolution: Run one complete charge-discharge cycle (charge to 100%, discharge to ~10%, recharge to 100%). The BMS will recalibrate the voltage-to-SOC curve and the gauge will report accurately thereafter.

LCD Backlight Occasionally Won't Wake

Intermittently the LCD backlight does not activate on the first button press. Pressing any button a second time reliably wakes the display.

Resolved in firmware v2.2.0 and newer.

Firmware update procedure: Connect to the EcoFlow app on your phone → navigate to Settings → Firmware Update → tap Update Now. Do not unplug the unit during the 3–8 minute update. Maintain a stable Bluetooth connection and keep the phone within 3 feet of the unit during the update process.

Battery Module Architecture

Cell configuration and monitoring precision.

Cell Arrangement
16S × N Parallel

16 parallel strings of LiFePO4 cells in series configuration per battery pack. The exact parallel count is manufacturer-disclosed. This configuration delivers the 48V nominal bus with high redundancy.

BMS Monitoring Precision
±10 mV per cell

Individual cell voltage is monitored to ±10 mV accuracy. Any cell drifting outside the configured voltage window triggers a soft alert before escalating to a hard E6 cell-fault.

Thermal & Protection
6 Sensors Per Pack

NTC thermistor sensors at pack-level strategic points (inlet, outlet, busbar). Over-temperature de-rates charge/discharge current in three stages before triggering E1.
