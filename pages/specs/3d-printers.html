Common Issues & Fixes

Bed Adhesion Problems

Clean PEI sheet with isopropyl alcohol (90%+). Increase first layer height by 0.05mm. Increase bed temp by 5-10°C.

Stringing / Oozing

Enable retraction (68mm for direct drive, 45mm for Bowden). Lower temperature by 5-10°C. Increase travel speed to 200mm/s+.

Nozzle Clogging

Cold pull method: Heat to 220°C, insert PLA, cool to 90°C, pull out. Use needle to clean nozzle opening.

Universal Maintenance Guide

Weekly Maintenance (10-15 minutes)

  • Clean build plate with isopropyl alcohol and lint-free cloth
  • Remove dust from fans and electronics with air compressor or canned air
  • Check belt tension (should deflect 57mm when pressed)
  • Inspect nozzle for buildup, wipe clean with brass brush

Monthly Maintenance (30-45 minutes)

  • Lubricate linear rails with PTFE grease or machine oil (light weight)
  • Clean leadscrews, apply light oil to prevent binding
  • Check all screws and bolts for tightness (do not over-tighten)
  • Perform bed leveling / mesh bed leveling procedure

First Layer Calibration Procedure

Step 1: Clean the Bed

Thoroughly clean with isopropyl alcohol and lint-free cloth. Finger oil is the #1 cause of adhesion failure.

Step 2: Print Calibration Pattern

Print a large square (150x150mm) single layer pattern at 20-25mm/s speed. Use your standard PLA settings.

Step 3: Evaluate the Result

Too Close

Lines are translucent, bed is scraping plastic. Z-offset needs to increase.

Perfect

Lines are fully squished together, consistent, no gaps.

Too Far

Gaps between lines, plastic not sticking. Z-offset needs to decrease.

Step 4: Adjust Z-Offset

Adjust in 0.05mm increments, re-test after each change. Be patient, small changes make big difference!
