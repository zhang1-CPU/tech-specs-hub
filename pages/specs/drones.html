Common Fault Codes

Critical motor or ESC faults require grounding the aircraft until resolved. Check local regulations (FAA Part 107, EASA) before operating UAVs in any airspace.
Code Description Severity Cause & Fix
MOTOR_HOT Motor Over-Temperature ?winding temp > 80 °C Critical Land immediately, cool 10 min, check motor bell for debris. Persistent = motor replacement.
ESC_ERR ESC Fault ?motor drive signal error Critical Ground aircraft. Run ESC calibration via DJI Assistant 2. Persistent = ESC board replacement.
BATT_CELL_ERR Battery Cell Imbalance > 0.1 V Critical Do not fly. Full charge/discharge ×2 to rebalance. Persistent = replace battery (hazmat disposal).
COMPASS_ERR Compass requires calibration Warning Move away from metal structures, re-calibrate in DJI Fly app.
IMU_ERR IMU offset out of range Warning Run IMU calibration on flat surface in DJI app. Wait 3 min before takeoff.

Expert Warning: Why Resetting Battery Cycles Is Dangerous

Community tutorials exist for resetting battery cycles using CP2112 debug boards connected to the BMS interface. Our lab testing reveals a critical finding:

Core Problem:

Resetting cycles does NOT restore the battery's physical-chemical activity.

Once Internal Resistance (IR) increases ?even with the cycle counter reset to zero ?high-current discharge (S-mode climbs) will still trigger voltage sag and forced shutdown.

Lab Test Case:

  • A Mavic 3 Pro battery after 300 cycles, reset to "0 cycles"
  • During S-mode full-throttle climb test: voltage dropped from 80% to 15% at 80% charge
  • Forced landing occurred mid-flight
  • Cell IR measured: 15mΩ (factory) ?32mΩ (after 300 cycles)

Conclusion: The BMS cycle count can be reset, but cell degradation is irreversible.

Do NOT attempt this on production equipment. This is a lesson learned from real testing.
