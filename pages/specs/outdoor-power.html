Battery Chemistry: LFP vs NMC

The single most important spec for long-term reliability and safety.

LFP Lithium Iron Phosphate

The chemistry used in every modern power station worth buying. No cobalt. Higher thermal stability. The real advantage is cycle life — an LFP pack will hold its rated capacity for 3-6x longer than an equivalent NMC pack.

Cycle life (to 80%):3,000 - 6,000
Thermal runaway:Very low
Energy density:~150 Wh/kg
Cobalt content:None
Self-discharge:~2%/month
NMC Lithium Nickel Manganese Cobalt

Found in older Jackery, Goal Zero, and budget models. Higher energy density (lighter weight) but worse cycle life, lower thermal stability, and higher self-discharge. Cobalt content raises ethical and safety concerns.

Cycle life (to 80%):500 - 1,200
Thermal runaway:Moderate
Energy density:~220 Wh/kg
Cobalt content:~15-20%
Self-discharge:~5%/month

Sizing Guide: What Size Do I Need?

Realistic runtime by device category. Figure out your Wh requirement first — then buy the next size up.

Rule of thumb

Sum the wattage of every device you want to run simultaneously, multiplied by the hours you need to run them. Then add 30% for inverter losses and surge. That's your minimum capacity in watt-hours (Wh).

Off-Grid Solar Sizing

How much solar panel wattage and battery bank capacity do you actually need?

Formula 1

Daily Wh ÷ Peak sun hours
= Panel wattage

Example: 2,400 Wh/day ÷ 5 hours = 480 W of solar panels. Round up.

Formula 2

Panel wattage × 2-3
= Recommended battery bank

This gives you 2-3 days of autonomy without sun. Critical for off-grid reliability.

Formula 3

Solar volts (Voc) must
match MPPT range

Connect panels in series to raise voltage. Most MPPTs accept 12-100V or higher.

What every spec sheet omits

The "peak sun hours" figure for your location is everything. In the contiguous US, this ranges from 3.5 (Pacific NW, winter) to 5.5 (Southwest, summer). Use the worst month figure — not the annual average — and you won't be stranded.

Brand Comparison

Every brand has a strategic focus. Know what you're buying.

EcoFlow

Fast charging

Industry-leading fast charging (AC wall plug to 80% in ~60 minutes on many models). Expandable ecosystem with extra batteries, smart panels, and home integration. Premium pricing.

Strengths:Speed, ecosystem
Weakness:Premium pricing

Jackery

Widely available

Simple UI, lightweight NMC/LFP packs, and available at every big-box retailer (Costco, Walmart, Amazon). Great for casual camping. Not the most feature-dense, but reliable and easy to return.

Strengths:Lightweight, ubiquitous
Weakness:Basic feature set

Anker SOLIX

Solid build

Strong inverter quality and good build engineering. Anker makes excellent consumer battery packs and that quality shows through in their power stations. Balanced across most categories.

Strengths:Build quality, inverter
Weakness:Slower than EcoFlow

Bluetti

Best $/Wh

Strong value per watt-hour. Their AC70P and AC200L models undercut comparable EcoFlow units by 30-40%. Heavier weight and slightly less polished firmware, but hard to beat on raw capacity.

Strengths:Best $/Wh ratio
Weakness:Heavier, simpler UI

Goal Zero

Legacy brand

The original outdoor-industry power station brand. Rugged enclosure, good warranty support. Still uses older lithium-ion chemistries on many models — which means lower cycle life than modern LFP competitors.

Strengths:Rugged, support
Weakness:Older chemistries
