CAT Safety Ratings - Why They Matter for HV Work

CAT I

Electronic equipment, signal circuits. NOT for mains work.

CAT II

Single-phase loads, plug-in equipment. Household outlets.

CAT III

3-phase distribution, fixed installation. Minimum for HV battery diagnosis.

CAT IV

Utility-level, outdoor service entrance. Maximum protection.

For Toyota Prius HV battery (207-260 VDC): use CAT III 1,000 V rated or better. Never use a CAT I or CAT II meter on HV battery circuits.

How We Evaluate Multimeters

Safety Rating Verification

We only list meters with independently verified CAT ratings - not self-certified claims. We cross-reference UL, ETL, TÜV, or CE certificates before inclusion. A $15 "CAT III 1000V" meter from an unknown brand typically has no real certification and should never be used on HV systems.

DC Accuracy Testing

We compare DC accuracy to published specifications and cross-reference against independent testing by EEVBlog, Kerry Wong, and similar independent electronics reviewers who verify against calibrated references.

Use-Case Matching

We prioritize meters that fit the use cases common to TechSpecsHub readers: HV battery diagnosis (needs CAT III 1000V+), power station testing (needs true-RMS), and electronics repair (needs high display count).

Price-to-Value Assessment

We evaluate each meter in context of what it costs to step up to the next tier. There is a measurable safety and accuracy gap between a $60 meter and a $4-$30 Fluke - we document it honestly so readers can make informed decisions.
