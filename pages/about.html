Our Mission

We believe technical data about consumer electronics should be accurate, discoverable, and free.

Too many spec sheets are either locked behind manufacturer paywalls or buried in SEO-spam affiliate sites that don't actually verify numbers. Too many reviewers open a box, say "it's heavy," and move on without measuring the thing people actually need to know — idle drain, real-world runtime, charge efficiency, firmware quirks, and what the error codes on the screen actually mean.

We publish the specs the manufacturers should be publishing — clearly, organized by device, with error codes and diagnostic procedures.

Editorial Standards & Data Verification

Six rules we follow on every page. If we can't meet them, we don't publish.

1. Primary Source Preference

Manufacturer service manuals, official data sheets, and regulatory filings (FCC, CE, UL) first. Third-party review sites are only used for cross-verification, never as the sole source of a spec claim.

2. Cross-Verification

Every spec that appears on TechSpecsHub is checked against at least two independent sources where possible — the manufacturer spec sheet, a service manual, a reviewer who measured the value, or our own measurement.

3. Disclose When Speculation

If we can only get one source for a claim, or if we're extrapolating from similar models, we say so explicitly. Look for phrases like "estimated from similar models" and "manufacturer-claimed (not independently measured)."

4. Version Tracking

When a manufacturer releases a hardware revision with different specs — extremely common with power stations — we track both versions explicitly. We don't silently change tables; we add rows and keep the old data visible.

5. Community Correction

Readers flag errors via our contact form. Every correction is logged, reviewed within 2-3 business days, and the relevant page is updated. We don't remove old data silently — updates are noted at the top of the page.

6. No Pay-to-Play

No paid listings. No sponsored rank positions in comparison tables. Affiliate revenue — when present — is disclosed on the page but does not influence spec content or model ordering. We have never accepted payment from a manufacturer in exchange for coverage.

Team & Contributors

A small team of engineers, solar installers, and consumer electronics reviewers. We don't have sales staff. We don't take advertising from the companies whose products we review.

Contributors join based on specific expertise — a licensed electrician for inverter specs, a drone pilot for gimbal calibration, a solar installer for off-grid system sizing. Every contributor signs the same no-pay-to-play policy.
