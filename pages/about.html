About TechSpecsHub

A utility-first technical reference built for engineers, repair technicians, and serious DIYers who need accurate data — not paraphrased forum guesses.

Our Mission

TechSpecsHub exists to eliminate the gap between official OEM documentation and the people who actually need it. When a Prius battery fails at 150,000 miles, the owner shouldn't have to scroll through 40 forum pages to find the correct internal resistance threshold. When an EcoFlow stops charging after two years, the fix shouldn't require a service appointment — it's usually a $0 app setting change.

We compile, verify, and structure technical data — cycle-life numbers, fault code definitions, measurement tolerances, diagnostic logic — into searchable, scannable reference pages that give you the answer in 60 seconds.

Data Sources

Official Service Manuals & TSBs

Manufacturer-issued technical service bulletins, repair manuals, and product datasheets are the primary source for all specification values. NHTSA.gov, official brand portals.

Community Verification

Data from PriusChat, r/EcoFlow, RCGroups, and iFixit teardowns is collected and filtered to statistically validate or challenge OEM claims with real-world results.

Lab & Academic Standards

Battery parameters are cross-referenced against ISO 12405 (Li-ion traction batteries), IEC 62133 (portable cell safety), and OSHA 1910.269 for high-voltage electrical safety.

Regular Re-Verification

Each data page carries a "Last Verified" date. Pages older than 6 months are flagged for review. Manufacturers occasionally revise specs after product releases.

Editorial Methodology

  1. 01

    Extract from Primary Sources

    Every spec table begins with official documentation. We use filetype:pdf searches against manufacturer domains, NHTSA.gov TSB databases, and FCC ID filings to locate primary source materials.

  2. 02

    Translate into Human-Readable Diagnostic Logic

    Technical parameters are converted into actionable troubleshooting steps — not just "battery voltage: 201.6V" but "measure cell groups; if any block reads below 14V, that block requires replacement."

  3. 03

    Visualize with Diagnostic Flowcharts

    Mermaid.js decision trees are built for every major fault pattern, showing the exact measurement sequence from symptom to root cause. Each branch references a specific parameter from the spec table.

  4. 04

    Cite and Date-Stamp Everything

    Every page ends with a Technical References block: primary source document, sample size for community data, applicable lab standard, and the date of last verification.
