What is Battery Temperature Error?

The Battery Temperature Error on drones indicates that the intelligent flight battery's internal temperature is outside the safe range for operation or charging. Lithium-polymer (LiPo) and lithium-ion drone batteries have narrow safe windows—typically 0C to 40C for discharging and 5C to 45C for charging. Flying in cold weather reduces chemical activity and voltage output, triggering low-temperature warnings. Flying or charging in hot conditions risks thermal runaway. The battery management system inside DJI and other smart batteries monitors cell temperature via thermistors and communicates limits to the aircraft. Repeated temperature errors can degrade cell life and in extreme cases cause swelling or venting. Proper storage, pre-flight conditioning, and mindful charging habits prevent most temperature-related issues.

Common Symptoms

  • App shows 'Battery Temperature Too Low' or 'Too High'
  • Drone refuses to take off or land immediately
  • Battery LED flashes red or yellow rapidly
  • Charging stops shortly after starting
  • Battery feels very cold or warm to the touch

Tools You'll Need

  • Insulated battery bag or case
  • Portable hand warmer (for cold weather)
  • Shade or cooling pack (for hot weather)
  • Infrared thermometer (optional)

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. 01
    Check the ambient temperature and compare it to the battery's rated operating range in the manual.
  2. 02
    If the battery is too cold, place it in a warm pocket or use a hand warmer outside the battery casing for 10-15 minutes.
  3. 03
    If the battery is too hot, move it to shade and allow it to cool for 15-30 minutes before flight or charging.
  4. 04
    Inspect the battery for swelling, punctures, or discolored cells. Do not use damaged batteries.
  5. 05
    Update the drone and battery firmware via the app; some updates refine temperature thresholds.
  6. 06
    Store batteries at room temperature (20C-25C) and 40-60% charge when not in use for extended periods.
  7. 07
    If temperature errors occur at normal ambient conditions, the internal thermistor may be faulty. (Based on common architecture; verify with your specific model manual)
  8. 08
    Contact the manufacturer for battery replacement if the error persists across multiple charge cycles.

When to Call a Professional

If the battery swells, smells unusual, or shows temperature errors at room temperature, discontinue use and seek a replacement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I fly in winter?
Yes, but warm batteries to at least 15C before takeoff. Cold batteries sag voltage and reduce flight time.
Why does my battery get hot while charging?
Fast charging generates heat. Use standard charge rates and charge in a ventilated area.
Is it safe to store batteries in the fridge?
Only if sealed in an airtight bag to prevent condensation. Room temperature storage is simpler and safer.
