What is Vision System Error?

The Vision System Error on DJI drones indicates that one or more visual positioning or obstacle avoidance sensors are not functioning correctly. DJI drones use downward, forward, backward, and sometimes lateral vision sensors to maintain position during hover, enable precision landing, and avoid obstacles. These sensors include cameras and infrared time-of-flight modules. Errors can be caused by dirty lenses, scratched sensor covers, poor lighting, reflective surfaces, firmware bugs, or hardware failure. Because the vision system is integral to flight safety, this error often restricts flight modes or disables obstacle avoidance until resolved. Cleaning and recalibration resolve most cases; hardware replacement is rare but necessary if the sensor module is damaged.

Common Symptoms

  • App displays 'Vision System Error' or specific sensor errors
  • Obstacle avoidance is disabled in settings
  • Drone drifts during hover indoors or at low altitude
  • Precision landing fails or landing is unstable
  • Error appears only in low light or over reflective surfaces

Tools You'll Need

  • DJI Fly or DJI GO 4 app
  • Microfiber cloth and lens cleaner
  • Cotton swabs and isopropyl alcohol
  • Bright, even lighting for calibration
  • Patterned floor or calibration mat (for downward sensors)

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. 01
    Power on the drone and check the app for specific sensor errors (forward, backward, downward, etc.).
  2. 02
    Inspect all vision sensor lenses and covers for dirt, fingerprints, scratches, or cracks.
  3. 03
    Clean gently with a microfiber cloth and lens cleaner. Avoid abrasive materials.
  4. 04
    For stubborn residue, use a cotton swab lightly dampened with isopropyl alcohol.
  5. 05
    Navigate to the vision system calibration section in the app. (Based on common architecture; verify with your specific model manual)
  6. 06
    Follow the on-screen prompts to calibrate each sensor. This usually involves pointing the drone at specific patterns or textures.
  7. 07
    If calibration fails, try a different environment with bright, diffuse lighting and textured surfaces.
  8. 08
    Update firmware and test flight in an open area to confirm obstacle avoidance and positioning are restored.

When to Call a Professional

If sensors are cracked, calibration fails in multiple environments, or the error persists after firmware updates, contact DJI support for sensor module replacement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I fly with a vision system error?
Yes in most cases, but obstacle avoidance and precision landing will be disabled. Fly cautiously.
Why does the error only happen indoors?
Downward sensors rely on texture and light. Glossy floors and dim rooms confuse the system.
Do ND filters cause vision errors?
ND filters cover the camera lens, not vision sensors. They do not affect obstacle avoidance.
