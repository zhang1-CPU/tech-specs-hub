What is E2 High Temp?

The E2 High Temperature error occurs when internal sensors detect that the battery pack, inverter, or charging circuitry has exceeded safe operating temperatures. Portable power stations use lithium-ion or LiFePO4 cells that degrade rapidly above 45-60 degrees Celsius depending on chemistry. To preserve battery life and prevent thermal runaway, the battery management system (BMS) reduces output or shuts down the unit entirely when thresholds are crossed. E2 is most common during simultaneous high-wattage discharging and charging, operation in direct sunlight, or use in enclosed spaces with poor ventilation. Some units also trigger E2 when the ambient temperature is simply too high for the passive or active cooling design to keep up.

Common Symptoms

  • Screen displays E2 or temperature icon
  • AC or DC output shuts down
  • Cooling fan runs at maximum speed if equipped
  • Unit feels hot to the touch
  • Error clears after cooling period

Tools You'll Need

  • Infrared thermometer (optional)
  • Shade or cover for outdoor use
  • Small fan for additional ventilation

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. 01
    Immediately reduce or remove all loads from the power station when E2 appears.
  2. 02
    Move the unit to a cool, shaded, well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight.
  3. 03
    If the unit has a cooling fan, ensure the intake and exhaust vents are not blocked.
  4. 04
    Allow the unit to cool for at least 15-30 minutes before attempting to use it again.
  5. 05
    Check firmware updates via the manufacturer's app; some updates refine thermal management thresholds.
  6. 06
    Avoid simultaneous high-wattage charging and discharging, which generates the most heat.
  7. 07
    If E2 occurs frequently in normal conditions, inspect vents for dust buildup and clean with compressed air. (Based on common architecture; verify with your specific model manual)
  8. 08
    If cleaning and ventilation do not resolve the issue, the internal temperature sensor or BMS may be faulty.

When to Call a Professional

If E2 appears at room temperature with no load, or if the unit does not cool down normally, contact the manufacturer for warranty service.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use my power station in hot weather?
Yes, but keep it shaded and allow airflow. Avoid continuous high loads above 35C ambient.
Does E2 damage the battery?
The shutdown protects the battery, but frequent high-temperature events reduce long-term lifespan.
Why does E2 happen while charging?
Charging generates heat. Fast charging in warm environments often triggers thermal limits.
