Ender 3 CR-Touch / BLTouch Error — Probe Troubleshooting Guide

CR-Touch and BLTouch are auto-bed leveling probes used on modified Creality Ender 3 printers to automatically measure and compensate for bed unevenness. This guide covers CR-Touch self-test abnormality diagnosis, probe pin stuck or damaged troubleshooting, 5-pin ribbon cable connection issues, CR-Touch mounting and alignment, manual leveling fallback procedures, and firmware updates required for CR-Touch support. Compatible with Ender 3 with CR-Touch kit, Ender 3 Pro with BLTouch, Ender 3 V2, Ender 3 S1, and Ender 3 S1 Plus.

What Usually Goes Wrong?

CR-Touch and BLTouch are precision electromechanical probes that use a spring-loaded pin and an electromagnetic solenoid to deploy and retract a sensing pin. The probe performs a self-test on power-up by deploying and retracting the pin automatically. If the pin fails to deploy, fails to retract, or the sensor does not detect the pin position correctly, the firmware will display an error and halt operation. Unlike simple limit switches, these probes have internal electronics and require specific firmware support. Most failures are related to the cables, connectors, or physical obstructions preventing pin movement rather than internal probe electronics failure.

  • • Ribbon cable connection failure: the 5-pin cable between the probe and mainboard can become loose, oxidized, or damaged — the #1 cause of probe errors.
  • • Probe pin obstruction: the spring-loaded pin can become jammed by debris, filament splatter, or if the probe is mounted too close to the nozzle.
  • • Firmware not configured for probe: if the firmware does not have BLTouch/CR-Touch support enabled, the probe signals will be misinterpreted.
  • • Incorrect probe mounting height: the probe pin must be within a specific height range relative to the nozzle tip for proper triggering.

Work through these steps in order. Most CR-Touch/BLTouch errors are resolved by reseating the ribbon cable or clearing debris from the probe pin mechanism.

Quick Symptom Check

Answer these before proceeding:

  • Does the probe pin deploy at power-on during self-test?
  • Does the red LED on the probe illuminate or blink?
  • Did you recently change the nozzle or hotend assembly?
  • Is this a new CR-Touch installation or did it work before?
  • What firmware version is currently installed?

Tools & Materials You'll Need

Electrical

  • Digital multimeter (for continuity testing)
  • Replacement 5-pin probe cable (if damaged)
  • Isopropyl alcohol 90%+ (for cleaning contacts)
  • Electrical tape (for cable strain relief)

Mechanical

  • Allen key set (1.5mm, 2mm for probe mount)
  • Small Phillips screwdriver
  • Needle or dental pick (for clearing debris from probe)
  • Compressed air duster (for cleaning probe mechanism)

Firmware

  • SD card with CR-Touch/BLTouch compatible firmware
  • Creality official firmware or Marlin with probe config
  • USB cable for firmware flashing (if applicable)
  • Z-offset configuration access via display or G-code

Diagnostic Tree — Work These In Order

01

Verify probe self-test behavior at power-on

When you power on the Ender 3 with CR-Touch or BLTouch installed, the probe should automatically deploy its pin 3-5 times in a self-test sequence. Watch carefully to see if the pin deploys downward, retracts up, and repeats. If the pin does not move at all, check the red LED on the probe body: a solid red LED indicates power is reaching the probe. A blinking red LED indicates a fault condition inside the probe. No LED at all means the probe is not receiving power — check the cable connection first.

CR-Touch / BLTouch LED Indicators
Red LED on solid: Power OK, probe ready
Red LED blinking: Self-test failed, probe fault
Red LED off: No power, check cable connection
Yellow LED: Probe is deployed and triggered (touching bed)
02

Reseat the 5-pin probe cable at both ends

The 5-pin ribbon cable connecting the CR-Touch/BLTouch to the mainboard is the most common failure point. Unplug the cable at both the probe body and the mainboard header. Inspect the metal pins for oxidation, bent pins, or pushed-out contacts. Clean the contacts gently with isopropyl alcohol and a soft cloth. Reinsert the cable firmly, ensuring it is fully seated in the connector. On the mainboard end, the cable must be inserted in the correct orientation — the colored wires typically face toward the board or toward the probe depending on the pinout. Reverse-plugging the cable will not damage the probe but will cause incorrect operation.

Cable orientation: the 5-pin cable has a keying notch that must align with the connector. Do not force the cable. If it does not insert easily, check the orientation. Never plug or unplug the probe cable while the printer is powered on — always power off first.

03

Inspect and clear probe pin mechanism

The spring-loaded probe pin can become jammed by small debris, dried filament residue, or if the probe body has accumulated plastic buildup from printing. Power off the printer, then manually push the probe pin up and down with a finger to check if it moves freely. If the pin is stuck, use a needle or dental pick to carefully clear any debris from around the pin bore. Do not use excessive force. Apply a very small amount of machine oil or silicone lubricant to the pin guide surfaces if needed. Blow out the probe body with compressed air to remove any loose particles.

Probe Pin Clearance Requirements
  • Pin must move freely up and down without binding
  • No filament residue on pin body or inside bore
  • Probe body not touching or hitting nozzle at any X/Y position
  • Minimum 2mm clearance between probe body and nozzle at lowest Z position
04

Check probe mounting height and alignment

The CR-Touch or BLTouch probe must be mounted at the correct height relative to the nozzle tip. When the probe pin is fully retracted (up position), the tip of the probe pin should be 1-3mm below the nozzle tip at the point where the probe is directly over the leveling points on the bed. If the probe is mounted too high, the pin will not make contact with the bed. If mounted too low, the pin may not deploy fully or may be pressed against the bed preventing retraction. Loosen the probe mount screws, adjust the height by rotating the probe body, then retighten while holding the position.

Mounting bracket note: Creality provides a specific mounting bracket for Ender 3 CR-Touch kits. Ensure the bracket is mounted to the X-axis gantry in the correct orientation and that the probe is centered over the hotend with adequate clearance at all positions on the bed. Some third-party brackets may have different mounting hole spacing.

05

Test cable continuity with multimeter

If reseating the cable did not resolve the issue, the cable itself may be damaged internally. The ribbon cable inside the 5-pin connector is thin and can break from repeated flexing as the X-axis moves. With the cable unplugged from both ends, use a multimeter in continuity mode to test each of the 5 wires from one end to the other. Any wire that shows no continuity (open circuit) must be replaced. Also check for shorts between adjacent wires. If the cable shows continuity at the connector ends but intermittent operation, the break may be inside the insulation and the cable should be replaced.

5-Pin Probe Cable Continuity Check
  • Pin 1 (GND): continuity from probe pin 1 to mainboard pin 1
  • Pin 2 (Z-MIN): signal pin — continuity check
  • Pin 3 (5V): power supply from board
  • Pin 4 (SERVO): deploy/retract control signal
  • Pin 5 (GND): second ground (some configurations)
06

Verify firmware has CR-Touch / BLTouch support enabled

If the probe hardware tests good but the printer still shows an error, the firmware may not be configured for the probe. Creality stock firmware on Ender 3 does not support CR-Touch or BLTouch out of the box — you must flash firmware that specifically includes probe support. Creality provides dedicated firmware for Ender 3 models with CR-Touch installed. Alternatively, Marlin firmware with BLTouch support can be compiled with the correct Configuration.h settings for your specific Ender 3 variant.

07

Perform manual bed leveling fallback procedure

If the CR-Touch/BLTouch probe cannot be fixed immediately and you need to continue printing, you can temporarily disable the probe and use manual bed leveling. In Marlin, send G-code M211 S0 to disable software endstops, then use G29 (if enabled) or manually level the four corners using paper test and BABYSTEP_ZPROBEING. In Creality stock firmware without probe support, you can use the existing tramming screws at bed corners and disable the auto-leveling G-code from your slicer start script. Remember to re-enable software endstops with M211 S1 after manual leveling.

  • Manual leveling steps: Home axes (G28), move to front-left corner, adjust bed screw until paper friction feels correct, repeat for all four corners, verify center point.
  • G-code for manual probe disable: M421 Q clears probe mesh, M211 S0 disables endstops temporarily.
  • Safety note: manual leveling removes bed mesh compensation. First layer adhesion may suffer and you may need to adjust Z-offset manually for each print.

Firmware-Specific Notes

Creality CR-Touch Firmware

Creality provides dedicated firmware files for Ender 3 models with CR-Touch already installed. These firmware versions have the probe pins pre-configured for the specific mainboard variant. Download the correct firmware from the official Creality website matching your exact Ender 3 model (Ender 3, Ender 3 Pro, Ender 3 V2, or Ender 3 S1) and mainboard revision. Flashing the wrong firmware file can result in probe non-operation or damage to the mainboard.

Marlin Firmware

In Marlin Configuration.h, you must enable BLTOUCH and define the probe pin with Z_MIN_PROBE_PIN. The deployment and retraction commands are defined by BLTOUCHDeploy and BLTOUCHMode commands. After flashing, run M502 to reset to defaults and M500 to save.

Klipper Firmware

In Klipper, the CR-Touch and BLTouch are configured using the [bltouch] section in printer.cfg. You must define the sensor_pin and control_pin. Klipper supports both 3.3V and 5V probes. Run PROBE_CALIBRATE to find the probe offset and BED_MESH_CALIBRATE to generate the bed mesh.

Z-Offset Configuration

After getting the probe operational, you must set the Z-offset (the distance between the probe trigger point and the nozzle touch point on the bed). In Marlin, use M851 Z0 followed by G28 and G29, thenbabystep or use M851 to set the measured offset. In Klipper, use PROBE_CALIBRATE and follow the guided procedure to set the Z-offset accurately.

When to Call a Professional or Replace Parts

Probe pin does not deploy at all and LED is off — cable or mainboard probe header damaged.

Probe LED blinks continuously during self-test — internal probe electronics fault, replace probe.

Probe pin deploys but never retracts — probe mechanism jam or solenoid failure, replace probe.

Ribbon cable tests show open circuit on multiple wires — replace the 5-pin probe cable.

If the printer is under warranty and CR-Touch installation was factory-original, contact Creality support.
