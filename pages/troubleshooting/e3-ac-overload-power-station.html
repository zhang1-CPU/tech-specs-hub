What is E3 AC Overload?

E3 AC Overload is similar to E1 but is specific to the AC inverter output on some power station brands. While E1 may indicate a general system overload, E3 is often triggered when the AC side specifically exceeds its rated capacity. Some manufacturers use E3 to distinguish between AC port overload and DC or total system overload. The root cause is the same: connected appliances draw more watts than the AC inverter can supply continuously. Resolving E3 involves identifying which AC outlet or appliance caused the trip, reducing the load, and resetting the inverter. It is important to distinguish between resistive loads like heaters and inductive loads like motors, which have high inrush currents that can fool simple wattage calculations.

Common Symptoms

  • E3 displayed on LCD or app
  • AC outlets lose power while USB/DC may remain active
  • Beeping alarm from the unit
  • Error repeats if the same appliance is reconnected
  • Unit may feel warm near the inverter section

Tools You'll Need

  • Wattage meter
  • Appliance nameplate or manual
  • Manufacturer app for load monitoring

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. 01
    Disconnect all AC appliances from the power station.
  2. 02
    Identify the continuous and surge ratings for your specific AC inverter.
  3. 03
    Measure each appliance's running and startup wattage using a meter.
  4. 04
    Reconnect appliances one by one, starting with the lowest wattage, while monitoring total load.
  5. 05
    Avoid running multiple high-wattage devices simultaneously on the same AC circuit.
  6. 06
    Reset the AC inverter by turning the AC output off and on, or via the app. (Based on common architecture; verify with your specific model manual)
  7. 07
    If E3 occurs with no load, the inverter current sensor may be faulty; contact support.

When to Call a Professional

If E3 triggers with zero AC load, or if the inverter smells burnt, seek manufacturer service. Internal inverter damage is not user-serviceable.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between E1 and E3?
E1 is often a general overload, while E3 is AC-specific on some brands. Check your manual for exact definitions.
Can I run a microwave on a power station?
Only if the microwave's running wattage is well below the inverter's continuous rating and surge capacity.
Why does my power station show E3 at half capacity?
Some units split AC output across multiple circuits. A single port may have a lower limit than total inverter capacity.
