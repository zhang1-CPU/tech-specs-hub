Severity: Critical Difficulty: Advanced 4-6 hours DIY $300-900 Dealer $2,000-4,500
Never open a high-voltage battery without proper training and PPE. A failed module can pose shock and fire hazards.

What is P3004?

P3004 indicates a specific fault within a high-voltage battery module, typically detected by the battery management system monitoring individual cell or block voltages, temperatures, or balancing circuits. This code is critical because a failed module can cause uneven charging, overheating, and in extreme cases thermal runaway. It appears in Toyota, Lexus, Honda, and Ford hybrid systems. Causes include internal cell shorting, broken cell interconnects, failed temperature sensors, or corrosion at module terminals. Diagnosis involves isolating the affected module through live data and physical inspection. Due to high-voltage risks, repairs must follow strict safety protocols including service plug removal, capacitor discharge wait times, and use of insulated tools.

Common Symptoms

  • Red hybrid warning triangle and check engine light
  • Immediate power reduction or limp mode
  • Battery temperature warnings
  • Uneven battery block voltages in live data
  • Burning or chemical odor from battery compartment

Tools You'll Need

  • OBD-II scanner with live battery block data
  • High-voltage insulated gloves and face shield
  • Digital multimeter with HV leads
  • Module extraction tools
  • Torque wrench
  • Thermal camera (optional but recommended)

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. 01
    Power off the vehicle, remove the key, and wait 10 minutes. Remove the HV service plug from the battery pack.
  2. 02
    Scan for codes and identify which battery block or module is flagged by the BMS.
  3. 03
    Remove the battery pack cover and visually inspect the flagged module for swelling, leaks, or burnt terminals.
  4. 04
    Measure the voltage of the flagged module and compare it to adjacent modules. A significant deviation confirms failure.
  5. 05
    If the module is replaceable, remove it using manufacturer-specified procedures and install a matched replacement. (Based on common architecture; verify with your specific model manual)
  6. 06
    Inspect the bus bars and nuts for corrosion; clean or replace as needed.
  7. 07
    Reassemble the pack, reinstall the service plug, and clear fault codes.
  8. 08
    Perform a slow charge-discharge cycle and monitor temperatures and voltages for stability.

When to Call a Professional

If thermal damage is visible, if multiple modules are failing, or if you do not have high-voltage training, this repair should be performed by a certified technician.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I replace just one module?
Yes, if the rest of the pack is healthy. Use a module with similar age and capacity to avoid imbalance.
What causes a module to fail?
Age, heat, deep cycling, and manufacturing defects are common causes.
Is P3004 dangerous?
It can be. A faulty module may overheat. Park the vehicle outdoors and avoid charging until repaired.
How do I match a replacement module?
Match voltage, capacity, and part number. Some rebuilders sell matched sets.
