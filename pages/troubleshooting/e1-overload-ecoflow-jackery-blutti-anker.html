What is E1 Overload?

The E1 Overload error is one of the most common faults reported on portable power stations from brands like EcoFlow, Jackery, Bluetti, and Anker. It occurs when the combined wattage of connected devices exceeds the inverter's continuous output rating. Most consumer power stations range from 300W to 3,600W continuous output. Surge loads from refrigerators, power tools, and motor-driven appliances can also trigger E1 even if their running wattage is within spec, because the initial inrush current briefly exceeds the inverter limit. The error is protective: it shuts down AC output to prevent overheating and damage. Understanding your device's wattage limits and how surge ratings differ from continuous ratings is key to preventing and resolving E1 errors.

Common Symptoms

  • AC output shuts off and E1 appears on screen
  • Audible alarm beep from the unit
  • Connected appliances lose power instantly
  • Error clears after reducing load and resetting
  • May occur intermittently with high-surge appliances

Tools You'll Need

  • Wattage meter or kill-a-watt device
  • Device manuals or nameplates for wattage ratings
  • Power station remote app (if supported)

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. 01
    Unplug all devices from the power station's AC outlets immediately when E1 appears.
  2. 02
    Check the continuous and surge wattage ratings printed on your power station or in its manual.
  3. 03
    Use a wattage meter to measure the actual draw of each appliance you plan to connect.
  4. 04
    Reconnect devices one at a time, ensuring the total running wattage stays below 80% of the continuous rating.
  5. 05
    For high-surge devices like refrigerators, consider a soft-start device or connect them to a higher-capacity unit.
  6. 06
    Reset the power station by holding the AC power button for 3-5 seconds or using the app reset function. (Based on common architecture; verify with your specific model manual)
  7. 07
    If E1 returns with no load, the inverter may have a fault; contact manufacturer support.

When to Call a Professional

If E1 triggers with no load connected, or if the inverter smells burnt or makes abnormal noises, the unit may need factory service or replacement under warranty.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does E1 happen even when my device says 500W and my station is 1000W?
Many devices have surge currents 2-3x their rated wattage at startup. The inverter sees the surge, not the average.
Can I bypass the overload protection?
No. The protection is hardware-based and essential for safety. Overloading risks fire and inverter damage.
Does E1 damage my power station?
No. E1 is a protective shutdown. Repeated overloads may stress components over time, so avoid frequent triggers.
