What is Gimbal IMU Calibration?

Gimbal IMU calibration is the process of teaching the drone's inertial measurement unit where level and neutral orientations are. The IMU combines accelerometer and gyroscope data to stabilize the camera and maintain horizon level. Over time, sensor drift, temperature changes, hard landings, or firmware updates can cause the IMU to develop offset errors. Symptoms include a tilted horizon, slow horizon drift during hover, or the gimbal failing to hold position. Calibration is a software procedure performed through the DJI app and requires a perfectly flat, vibration-free surface. It is one of the safest and most effective first steps when experiencing gimbal behavior issues, and it costs nothing but a few minutes of time.

Common Symptoms

  • Horizon is visibly tilted in photos and video
  • Gimbal slowly drifts left or right during hover
  • Gimbal does not return to center when double-tapping the app
  • Calibration prompt appears in the app
  • Jerky corrections after yaw inputs

Tools You'll Need

  • DJI Fly or DJI GO 4 app
  • Flat, level surface like a table or tile floor
  • Fully charged drone battery

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. 01
    Ensure the drone battery and remote controller are fully charged to prevent interruption.
  2. 02
    Power on the drone and remote, and connect to the app.
  3. 03
    Navigate to Settings > Control > Gimbal Calibration or IMU Calibration depending on your model.
  4. 04
    Place the drone on a perfectly flat, vibration-free surface. Avoid carpets or soft mats.
  5. 05
    Remove the gimbal protector/clamp so the gimbal can move freely during calibration.
  6. 06
    Start the calibration and do not touch or move the drone until the app confirms completion.
  7. 07
    If the app reports calibration failure, power cycle the drone and try a different surface.
  8. 08
    After successful calibration, verify by slowly panning the drone and checking horizon level in the app.

When to Call a Professional

If calibration fails repeatedly on multiple surfaces, the IMU hardware may be defective and require DJI service.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I calibrate the gimbal IMU?
Only when you notice drift or after hard landings and firmware updates. Over-calibration is unnecessary.
Can I calibrate on grass?
No. Soft or uneven surfaces introduce errors. Use a hard, level floor or table.
Why does my horizon still tilt after calibration?
Check that the gimbal roll axis is not physically damaged. Persistent tilt may require mechanical adjustment.
